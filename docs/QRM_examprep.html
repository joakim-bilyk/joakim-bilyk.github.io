Examination questions

The exam question are:

  1. Risk measures and axioms; theoretical properties of VaR and ES; loss r.v.’s and operators.
  2. VaR and ES: calculational methods (Var-Cov method, empirical methods, exact confidence intervals for VaR, Monte Carlo and importance sampling, bootstrap).
  3. Extreme value theory (and its general relationship to problems in risk management).
  4. Elliptical distributions.
  5. Copulas, I: Basics, Sklar’s Theorem, measures of dependence, statistical fitting to data.
  6. Copulas, II: Examples (e.g. Gaussian, elliptical, t, Archimedean, simulating Archimedean copulas), Frechet bounds.
  7. Credit risk, I: Merton’s model and its extensions (i.e. KMV, multidimensional Merton).*
  8. Credit risk, II: Portfolio credit risk management; “reduced form models” including the various mixture models.*

*In these shorter topics, you can also try to briefly relate these topics to themes from earlier in the course (VaR or copulas, for example), and the relationship between topics 7 and 8.
